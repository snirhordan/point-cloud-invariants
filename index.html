Point Cloud Invariants

A walkthrough of Harm Derksen's note proving a lower bound on the degree of separating invariants: for the symmetric group \(S_n\) acting on symmetric \(n\times n\) matrices (weighted graphs under relabeling), any separating set of homogeneous invariants must contain one of degree \(\gtrsim n^2/4\). Everything Harm cites is proved here from scratch.

Main Theorem Let \(V_n=S^2(\R^n)\) be the space of symmetric \(n\times n\) matrices, with \(S_n\) acting by \(X\mapsto P_\sigma X P_\sigma^{-1}\). Let \(\beta_{\mathrm{sep}}(K[V_n]^{S_n})\) be the smallest \(d\) such that separating invariants of degree \(\le d\) exist. Then \[ \liminf_{n\to\infty}\ \frac{\beta_{\mathrm{sep}}(K[V_n]^{S_n})}{n^2}\ \ge\ \frac14 . \]

The logic in one glance

The whole argument is a chain of five links. Each is proved on the pages that follow.

  1. Molien. The invariant ring's Hilbert series is \(H(K[V]^G,t)=\tfrac1{|G|}\sum_{g}\tfrac1{\det(I-t\rho(g))}\). (Background)
  2. Universal denominator. Set \(p(t)=\lcm_g \det(I-t\rho(g))\); then \(p(t)\,H\) is a polynomial. (Theorems)
  3. Divisibility. If homogeneous invariants \(f_1,\dots,f_r\) of degrees \(d_1,\dots,d_r\) have common zero set \(\{0\}\), then \(p(t)\mid\prod_i(1-t^{d_i})\). Proved by an equivariant Koszul complex. (Theorems)
  4. Separating \(\Rightarrow\) zero set \(\{0\}\). So the divisibility applies to any separating set. (Background / Theorems)
  5. A cyclotomic factor. A cleverly chosen \(\sigma\in S_n\) makes \(\det(I-t\rho(\sigma))\) divisible by \(1-t^{pq}\) for primes \(p,q\approx n/2\). The irreducible factor \(\Phi_{pq}\), of degree \((p-1)(q-1)\), must divide a single \(1-t^{d_i}\), forcing \(\max_i d_i\ge (p-1)(q-1)\approx n^2/4\). (Application)
