Point Cloud Invariants

A self-contained walkthrough of Harm Derksen's note Point Cloud Invariants (v2): degree bounds for separating invariants of weighted graphs (\(S_n\) on symmetric matrices) and of point clouds (\(O(d)\times S_n\) on \(d\times n\) matrices). Every cited black box is proved here (one is honestly sketched), and every mathematical error found in the note is flagged and repaired — see the Audit.

Main results at a glance

Graphs — a sandwich For \(S_n\) acting on \(V_n=S^2(\R^n)\) (weighted graphs under relabeling), \[ \Big(\tfrac14-o(1)\Big)\,n^2\ \le\ \beta_{\mathrm{sep}}\big(K[V_n]^{S_n}\big)\ \le\ \frac{(n^2-n)(n^2-n+2)}{8}\ \sim\ \frac{n^4}{8}. \] Lower bound: Molien / universal denominator / cyclotomic factor. Upper bound: Lagrange interpolation, improved by Göbel. (The note's upper constant has an arithmetic slip, corrected here.)
Point clouds For \(O(d)\times S_n\) acting on \(\R^{d\times n}\) (\(n\) points in \(\R^d\) up to rotation/reflection + relabeling), the separation degree satisfies, for \(n\ge3\), \[ D(n,d)\ \le\ 2N_d(n^2+n-1)\ \sim\ 2dn^3,\qquad N_d=dn-\tbinom d2 \qquad\text{(proved — the first-\(d\)-rows theorem)}. \] This repairs the note's broken Cholesky argument via the Gram completion identity (Geometric Graphs), improving the earlier \(\sim n^4/4\) Gram + Göbel bound. The note's stronger claim \(D(n,d)\le 2N_d(2N_d-1)=O(d^2n^2)\) remains open (Cholesky coordinates are neither permuted by \(S_n\) nor polynomial).

The logic in one glance

Lower bound (quadratic)

  1. Molien: \(H(K[V]^G,t)=\tfrac1{|G|}\sum_g\tfrac1{\det(I-t\rho(g))}\). (Background)
  2. Divisibility: separating \(\Rightarrow\) zero set \(\{0\}\) \(\Rightarrow\) \(\det(I-t\rho(g))\mid\prod_i(1-t^{d_i})\) for all \(g\) — proved via the equivariant Koszul complex. (Machinery)
  3. Cyclotomic factor: \(\sigma=(p\text{-cycle})(q\text{-cycle})\) with primes \(p,q\approx n/2\) gives \(1-t^{pq}\mid\det(I-t\rho(\sigma))\); irreducibility of \(\Phi_{pq}\) forces \(\max_i d_i\ge(p-1)(q-1)\approx n^2/4\). (Lower Bound)

Upper bounds (quartic)

  1. Lagrange: interpolate on the entry values of two non-equivalent matrices, symmetrize over \(S_n\): separation in degree \(N(2N-1)\sim n^4/2\). (Upper Bound §1)
  2. Göbel: permutation actions are generated in degree \(\max\{m_i,\sum\binom{m_i}2\}\); with variable-orbits of sizes \(n\) and \(\binom n2\): \(\beta_{\mathrm{sep}}\le\frac{(n^2-n)(n^2-n+2)}{8}\sim n^4/8\). (Upper Bound §2)

Point clouds

  1. Gram reduction: \(\Phi(X)=X^TX\) identifies \(O(d)\times S_n\)-orbits with \(S_n\)-orbits of rank-\(\le d\) PSD matrices — losslessly, by Weyl's FFT. (Point Clouds §2)
  2. Transfer: \(D(n,d)\le 2\,\beta_{\mathrm{sep}}(K[V_n]^{S_n})\sim n^4/4\). (Point Clouds §5)
  3. First-\(d\)-rows repair: the completion identity puts every witness in the first \(d\) rows — \(N_d\) honest entries — giving \(D(n,d)\le 2N_d(n^2+n-1)\sim2dn^3\), validated in exact arithmetic. (Geometric Graphs)
  4. Open: the claimed \(O(d^2n^2)\) via Cholesky coordinates — proof gapped. (Point Clouds §4, Audit #5)
